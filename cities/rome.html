Together we can make Rome resilient to climate changes!

Urban resilience is the key to urban wellbeing: driving positive change through knowledge sharing

Ancient roots with an eye to the future

 Rome was already a metropolis 2000 years ago and is now home to a population of nearly 3 millions residents, and daily hosting 2 million commuters and 125.0000 tourists. Rome, as any other modern large city, faces the pressures of people moving from the hinterland to the city center, but in an urban context which results from the stratification of subsequent ages, and still keeps the original footprint of the ancient Roman structure. This at the same time represents an invaluable cultural resource and a strong constraint to new urbanistic solutions. Resilience is not only the ability to adapt  to external factors, it is also the ability to assess weaknesses and disclose opportunities, and to implement innovative actions  for an effective  governance of urban services,  and for the design of sustainable public policies.
