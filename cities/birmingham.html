Climate change measures simply aren’t working in Birmingham and are hitting our poorest hardest.

We want to engage with our citizens to accelerate Birmingham’s work in helping tackle climate change and encourage everyone who lives here to think and behave differently, and together address the environmental and well-being issues we all face.

We desperately need our citizens collaborating to help shape and create a greener place to live, work and play in. At the end of the day it is the people who live in Birmingham who should have the biggest say about how they want to tackle climate change issues.

Join us in an immersive 24 hours in the life of Birmingham City. Experience the reality of what communities wake up to, travel, work and play in. Talk to people, participate in some of the things they do, to really understand the challenges being faced and see what the barriers to the behaviour change are.  

Using different modes of transport, we'll be moving around the city to understand the real issues for people most affected by under strain environments. After each stopping point, we'll be pausing to contemplate and start to shape ideas based on meeting people that are being impacted every day.

What's in it for me?

  • Me
  • My city
  • The world

TAWS will give you a whirlwind tour of 24 hours in the life of Birmingham City - the likes of which you are unlikely to have experienced before.

On the day we will surround with experiences, residents, academics, service providers, activity providers, volunteers, politicians and anything else we can think will prompt thought and discussion - all there to help you challenge the "norm" and help us create some innovative but practical solutions to our challenges.

You will leave after 24 hours exhausted but stimulated and hopefully committed to helping tackle climate change here and anywhere else you come across it.

We know Birmingham City needs help. We need citizens and communities to get involved. It’s not about being told what to do but agreeing what to do. Together, let's find ways to impact climate change and improve wellbeing at a grass roots level. This is a chance to support a huge shift in the city, helping to find solutions to tackle climate change that benefit our poorest communities rather than hitting them hardest.  #8To80Wellbeing

You never know, if with your help we find ways to get it right, we can share what works in our incredibly diverse communities with other poor communities from across the globe to encourage and help them find practical and positive ways to tackle their climate change issues.

Schedule of the day

Check out our schedule for the 24-hours

7AM

City sunrise and start to move

Qi gong session in Alpha Tower, watching the sun come up and the city start to move

8.00AM

Welcome and introduction to the day

Find out what's in store while taking in some street food breakfast

9.15AM

Community Meet 1 - Children and Parents

Understand challenges being faced by parents in our communities with a visit to Mandela School

11.15AM

Community Meet 2 - On your bike

Take part in a learn to ride session and meet people from the community getting on their bikes

1PM

Real Junk Food lunch

Time to take in and chat about what you've seen so far over lunch at one of our Wellbeing Centres

2.45PM

Community Meet 3 - Older generation

Engage with older generations within the community, understand their challenges and concerns when talking environment

4PM

Community Meet 4 - Active Streets Closure

Hear what some of our residents have to say during one of our Active Street closures

5.00PM

City walking tour

See first hand the challenges being faced by commuters and people travelling around the city at the end of the working day. Walk to the centre for our evening session

5.45PM

Reflection session

Time to reflect and rest up for a bit

6.15PM

Working Session

Discuss what you've seen so far and start to structure some ideas

7.00PM

Ideas Tech Hack

join us for motivational round table discussions, watch videos from the day and collaboratively frame the identified challenges for the hackathon

9.30PM-7AM

Hackathon

Using the problems framed from the day, target tech experts to come up with digital solutions to overcome barriers in communities

Prizes

By taking part in our hackathon, you'll have the chance for your pitches to be shared with organisations that can help to turn ideas into reality.

Our expert judging panel will select the best idea from the hack session of the day based on how well it could impact issues raised within communities to address climate change issues and facilitate happier, healthier communities.
