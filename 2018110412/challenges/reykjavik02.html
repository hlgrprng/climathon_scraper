How can we turn towards a sustainable food management in Reykjavík?

Wasted food is a growing problem in our modern society and an untapped opportunity. Food waste occurs in all stages of the value chain of manufactured food products: from the production line, the transportation system, the sales, up to the consumer consumption.

Challenge context

It is estimated that greenhouse gas emissions from food waste in Iceland can be more than 200 Gg per year. That represents about 5% of Iceland's annual total emissions in 2013 and more than 8% from emissions emitted from activities covered by the European Union's Emissions Trading Scheme (ETS). The loss of food waste is therefore higher than can be attributed annually to, for example, the use of refrigerant in Iceland (171 Gg), and just under half of the emissions attributable to fishing (473 Gg).

Environmental impact derives from this food waste are significant and needs to be adressed.

Challenge description

Solutions should focus on:

  • Finding an attractive way to collect information from the inhabitants on their food waste
  • Integrating the up-stream environmental impact of the food production and not just the consumption
  • Interacting logically with the reseller: grocery and retail stores
  • Taking into consideration the agricultural ressources and potentials of Iceland
  • Residences (single and multi-family);Food-service entities (restaurants, cafeterias, etc.);Institutions (hospitals, universities, prisons, etc.)
