Together we can help Edinburgh transform its city centre!

Edinburgh is developing an action plan for a vibrant and people-focused city centre to improve community, economic and cultural life, as part of a wider vision for a clean, green, vibrant city.

Join us in Edinburgh to help city officials, partners and citizens to explore how we can source and use data and information on how people experience the city centre, to feed in to the wider city consultation about redeveloping the city centre to be people centred, reduce congestion and vehicle-borne air pollution, improve journey times by public transport, realise lifelong health benefits of active travel, and create public speaces that support city living.

Turn this vision into reality by working together with entrepeneurs, planners, students and city officials to provide workable solutions to understanding how people experience the city centre public realm  - and how we can improve these city centre public spaces through this redevelopment.

Come with an existing idea, or come and work with an inspiring team to develop solutions. Experts and mentors will be on hand to support teams through the day.

Challenge

How can we source - and use - data and information on the experiences people feel when they are in and around the city centre? We have lots of "system facts" about where and how people move around the city centre, but little information on how people experience the city centre public realm.

What do people experience in the city centre? How do we capture and use this data and information?

There is extensive work seeking to access and use data on how people move in and around the city centre - by foot, bike, car, tram, bus or taxi. But to design a resilient, zero carbon city, we need to understand how people experience public spaces so we can transform Edinburgh's public realm.

What's in it for me?

  • Me
  • My city
  • The world

The benefit of being part of a wider conversation about transforming Edinburgh's city centre, offering your ideas and working in a team with different skills, to help influence a place designed with people at its heart.

Edinburgh is one of the fastest growing cities in the UK, by 2040 it will have a population of almost 600,000. At the same time, the way we travel, shop, socialise, work and play is also changing, reflecting global trends and new technologies.

Edinburgh's growth will provide new jobs, homes and amenities, but it must be balanced to provide a high quality of life, access to services and opportunities for all residents.

Creating people-centred places - reducing congestion, air-pollution, improving health benefits of active travel, and creating public spaces that support city living - are key to achieve the Edinburgh 2050 vision of a cleaner, fairer, thriving, connected and inspired city.
