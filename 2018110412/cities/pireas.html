Fishing marine litter (macroplastics) to recycle/reuse in Piraeus

How can new technologies, coordinated action of various actors at local level and a shift to circular economy be combined to stimulate fishermen to fish macroplastics and bring them to shore while creating recycling/reuse opportunities at city level?

Piraeus is a port city located within the Athens urban area in Greece. The Port of Piraeus is the largest Greek seaport and one of the biggest in the Mediterranean and Europe servicing about 20 million passengers annually. 

Piraeus has developed a Blue Growth Strategy 2018-2024, main axes of which are:

  • blue entrepreneurship and innovation
  • integration of port functions with urban functions and uses
  • tackling the impacts of climate change on coastal urban areas
  • circular economy (with emphasis on waste from restaurants, cruise ships, and so forth)
  • the reduction of CO2 emissions
  • the digitization of the services of the Municipality
  • upgrading human skills and the position of enterprises in value chain systems. 

In this context, Piraeus joins for the first time the Climathon initiative aiming at solutions concerning the issue of marine plastic litter.

Prizes

Win support to further develop your solution, access to trainings ...or get the unique experience of fishing plastic !

The winning team and the participants of Climathon Piraeus will receive the following prizes:

  • Ticket for the incubation programme of BlueGrowth Piraeus (www.bluegrowth.gr). The services include:
      • Advisory support and training
      • Access to the BlueGrowth Marinescape (a lively human ecosystem of experts, mentors and investors in the Blue Economy) 
  • Up to 3 teams will be eligible for incubation services by the Athens Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation (ACEin), the incubation center of Athens University of Economics and Business. The services include:
      • Advisory support and training to help the teams’ further develop their idea
      • Access to ACEin’s network of experts and mentors
      • A friendly and lively office space to (www.acein.aueb.gr)
      • Full access to the SMIRES Training course 2019on the Economics of Sustainable Water Management of Intermittent Rivers and Ephemeral Streams in accordance to the Water Framework Directive (WFD), the Millennium Ecosystems Assessment (MEA) and Sustainable Development Goals of the UN Agenda 2030. The course will be held in Athens on the 4th - 6th of February, 2019.
  • Fishing tour to experience how plastic is daily captured in fishing nets, organized by Enaleia (www.enaleia.com)
  • Set of coasters by PHEE (https://phee.gr/). PHEE designs and manufactures unique natural products by recycling the leaves of seagrass Posidonia Oceanica.
